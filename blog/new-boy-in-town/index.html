New Boy in Town

October 24, 2020 · 2 min read

Banzi Kubheka

Junior Software Development

In one sentence, life at Glasswall has been great. The environment is relaxed, and the people are easy-going, making it easier to be productive. I have worked in places where the office environment is quite rigid and tense, and I've been in meetings where instead of finding solutions to problems, blame is being assigned, and that blame culture leads to low levels of productivity as people wouldn't even take initiatives in fear of being blamed when something goes wrong.

When I joined Glasswall, they were under immense pressure to meet a deadline and at no time was the environment hostile or was blame being assigned. This has been by far the best teamwork and collaboration I have seen, I mean the daily stand-ups alone show this. Anyone facing issues can just state it, by the time the stand-up is done, there are already volunteers to help solve that problem.

All work and no play makes Jack a dull boy as the saying goes, and there's no dullness in this place. There's table tennis available for all to play and have a friendly game just to unwind a bit, there are card games available as well. This I feel facilitates team bonding as well as building that unity so that no groups or team members feeling like outsiders.

Communication with the higher management is quite amazing in this place. The CEO holds weekly update meetings about the dealings of the company, potential contracts, potential changes, so everyone is in the loop about what is happening, which I feel motivates people as they have a full picture of what is happening.

In my years as a Project Engineer, I've worked in over eight different offices and none of them come close to being in such a non-hostile and easy going environment. From the perspective of someone who's not only new to the company, but new to the industry as a whole, the working environment allows for growth. The flexibility, as well of start and finish times (Flexitime) is another plus. So far, I haven't a single complaint this about place and I'm quite enjoying life at Glasswall.
