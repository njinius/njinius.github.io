Why haven't you written a blog yet?

August 1, 2021 · 8 min read

Darrel Barreto-White

Software Developer

"That's a good idea, maybe write a blog about it, I'm sure other people will probably run into the same issue

I often stared at my laptop screen within the same four "covid-restricted" walls asking myself why I haven't started blogging, despite the intermittent moments of enthusiasm to create content. These moments are often throated by the same questions clanging through my mind like a broken record:

// "I've wanted to write one for a while now, but why haven't I?"
// "I could probably put my thoughts in a blog. Why have I now got writer's block?"
// "I couldn't find that solution online; that should probably be added to a blog!"

..."Why do I keep making excuses?" "What am I afraid of?"...

One phrase I read online eventually settled in my mind...

"Actions lead to motivation."

Through this, an idea bubbled up through the stew of self-doubt. What if I try to answer that question through action? You might have discerned what I meant by this from the title of this blog.

The real why#

As I contemplated my "eureka" moment, I realised it isn't so much a question of why I haven't written a blog, instead "Why do I want to start blogging in the first place?". It's only until I break this question down that I finally start making some sense of my dilemma. An easy answer to this question could be... "Why not?" - and as courageous as that response sounds, it's unfortunately not enough to turn the tides.

To understand this question better, I started to list reasons for wanting to start blogging in the first place:

  • Getting better at writing in general
  • As a software developer - to share technical solutions for problems others might also encounter
  • To reflect on my thoughts and ideas (as I'm doing as I write this now)
  • To release that inner voice, however large or small it may turn out to be
  • To leave some of my thoughts and ideas behind:
    • for family and friends
    • for colleagues and acquaintances
    • or simply a stranger that's interested in what I wrote?!
  • To encourage the people around me to express themselves freely, if not through blogging, then maybe through other forms of expression. Painting? Music? or piece of code they'd like to share?

Breaking down the "why"#

Improving writing & reading skills#

"Practice makes perfect" or "Practice makes permanent" take your pick. We all get better at something the more we do it. It's fundamentally how the brain works. Repetition builds stronger neural connections, and in this case, blogging will strengthen my writing and reading ability over time (I assume). As I peer through the crystal ball at a future filled with my thoughts and ideas, I already see myself telling someone, "My blogs are decent now, but take a look at my first one.. it sucked!" - depending on who I'm talking to :P

Sharing solutions#

Software developers are problem solvers; that's what makes us tick. We're constantly looking for better and more efficient ways to solve an issue and ultimately learn something new in the process. The laziest among us (me) often try to automate repetitive tasks, allowing for quicker access to root causes. I'm a generalist through and through, which means I often write code in a wide range of frameworks and technologies either through the company I chose to work for or personally. Today it's Python, tomorrow it's PHP, then it's C# with a hint of React and SQL. So what comes along with the generalist? Yes, heaps to learn and a barrage of new problems to solve. When looking at a new framework or technology, I often overcome issues I couldn't find a complete solution to.

In many cases, I've had to open multiple blogs and articles to solve the problem. So why not share my complete solutions? If it helps at least one person, I consider that a success!

Sharing experiences#

"I've moved house 16 times, lived in 9 cities, within 4 countries and 2 continents."

We've all gone through a lot of experiences in life, from happy to sad. Having experienced different cultures, situations and problems throughout my life, I've accumulated a wealth of experience in many areas, which I'm sure you may also have. So why not share it? Expressing our experiences can latch onto someone out there that needs that extra push at the right time or simply provide a pleasant afternoon read. I've also been helped by others in times of great stress - it was exactly what I needed at the time. As mentioned above, we can share experiences in many ways; maybe you want to produce comics? I often read webcomics like "Cyanide and Happiness" and "xkcd" - a couple of silly cartoons now and then can brighten up my day.

xkcd : 1228

Self Reflection#

Self-reflection is a powerful tool to engage with our thoughts and ideas. Often it is done when we're faced with an issue or sensitive situation. As I write this, I'm allowing myself to think, rephrase and reflect on my thoughts and ideas. Often reflection will give us a new perspective on situations or prevent us from acting in haste. For example, that text message I wrote in anger but hesitated to send: self-reflection helped make the right decision in the end (or so I think). Maybe after some further self-reflection, I'll conclude this blog is a silly idea and "bin" it (by now, you know the answer to that)

Encourage others#

We've all been hypnotically engaged in an intriguing dialogue with someone that's captured our full attention with their excellent ideas and insights. "We could talk for hours" or "You should write a book" is an example of common phrases that protrude from such conversations. I don't consider myself the most exciting or interesting person, not even close - but I can certainly attest to other individuals that I find incredibly interesting. I'd like to encourage some of those people to bestow us with their phenomenal ideas and have a shot at "Writing a book blog!"

Breaking down the "why not"#

To avoid changing the title of this blog to "Why haven't you written a book yet" - I'll briefly touch on my "why not."

Judgements#

A lot of people shy away from self-expression. Sometimes it's because we're afraid of what others might think. When we expose ourselves to the rest of the world, we feel like it puts a "dent in our armour". Fears of judgment and rejection can leave us motionless, in a rut of self-doubt and concern. Sometimes we simply don't want to risk looking foolish in front of the masses.

Mistakes#

Many of the hindrances we see when building self-confidence can be related to the fear of making mistakes. We all make mistakes; that's a part of life - "Fail fast, fail forward" comes to mind. In a lot of cases, this understanding aids in building confidence considerably. I sometimes constrict my thoughts before making a decision or speaking up in group discussions, while others swiftly act on their "gut feel". Though we may have differing views of what a confident person is or looks like, I think we can all agree that fear of making an error is not in the list of traits. Realising that we will make mistakes in all our choices helps break through that confidence barrier. I will blunder when writing this blog! There are many reasons why we might lack self-confidence. Recognising them and reflecting on them helps break through the roadblocks. Maybe I'll share some of my more profound thoughts on this area in another post?

Excuses#

Procrastination will always get mentioned when you're not productive. It will always be present in everything we do. Everyday life is riddled with it - if you blink, you might miss it! I believe it feeds on my "why not". When self-doubt and fear creep in, procrastination latches on like a shoal of piranhas and all the excuses float to the top. Self-sabotage is one of the critical causes!

"Actions lead to motivation."#

Sometimes we just need to take action and let the results guide us forward.

I've found this little journey incredibly liberating. I observed myself holding back on details to try and keep everything relatively short - I think that's a positive sign for the future!

You might not want to write a blog; that just happens to be my dilemma. Maybe you'd like to share your recipes, paintings, music or code. When you're staring at your screen, pen and paper or maybe into the clouds contemplating sharing some of your thoughts and ideas - take the first step and see where it takes you.

Ultimately, I'll conclude with a question...

Why haven't you written a blog yet?
